Save the Bees

Heirloom Pollen

The Missing Puzzle Piece

In all the discussions about the dwindling bees, what is never mentioned is the difference between hybrid pollen and heirloom pollen. Is there a difference? Of course there is. One is manufactured and man-made, the other is natural.

If you were alive in 1903, you would have been able to choose from more than 500 varieties of cabbage, 400 varieties of peas and tomatoes, and 285 varieties of cucumbers?

By 1983, after the privitization of seeds, due to the introduction of hybrid seeds, the varieties available were down to 28 varieties of cabbage, 25 varieties of peas, 79 varieties of tomatoes, and just 16 varieties of cucumbers. Researchers found 93 percent of seeds were lost over eight decades.

By late 1990's, beekeepers starting reporting a reduction in the number of bees and a decline in beehives.

At Save the Bees Grow Heirloom Seeds, we believe that once the heirloom seeds disappeared, it took a little bit of time for the bees to be effected by the poor quality food source hybrid seeds provide, but subsequent generations of bees have been affected negatively by this poor quality food source. By the 1990s, the effects were noticeable.

We believe that to save the bees, nont only do we have to grow organically, we have to provide them with pollen from heirloom seeds.
