Save the Bees

All Seeds Are Not Created Equal

Hybrid seeds produce prolifically and provide uniform produce, but are generally low in taste. Heirloom seeds provide odd-shaped produce that are extremely tasty. Why is the heirloom produce tasty? Because it contains the highest level of nutrition available.

Your goal growing heirloom seeds for bees is to get the heirloom plants to blossom, because the pollen provides the bee food. If you want to use the produce, that's up to you.

How much time is involved?

You can put as much time as you want into growing food for bees. Setting things up the box, getting the seedlings going takes a little bit of time, but after you put the seedlings in the ground, the effort is minimal. Watering a few times a week, depending on moisture needs in your area, is the time involved. It does not take a lot of water to keep plants going.

One of the benefits of growing food for bees is that you can enjoy the produce it provides. There is no better food available than home-grown heirloom produce.
