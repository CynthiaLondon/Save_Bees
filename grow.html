Grow Heirloom Seeds

Save the Bees

All Seeds Are Not Created Equal

Hybrid seeds produce prolifically and provide uniform produce, but are generally not high in taste quality. Heirloom seeds provide odd-shaped produce which is tasty. The reason heirloom produce is so tasty is that heirloom produce provides the highest form of nutrition available.

To grow heirloom seeds for bees the plnats must be allowed to go to seed and blossom, because the pollen provides the bee food. If you want to use the produce, that's up to you. Most people elect to eat the produce.

How much time is involved?

You can put as much time as you want into growing food for bees. Setting things up a garden box and getting the seedlings going takes a little bit of time, but after you put the seedlings in the ground, the effort is minimal. Time involved after set up is watering a few times a week, depending on moisture needs in your area, and keeping the box weed-free. It does not take a lot of water to keep plants going.

One of the benefits of growing food for bees is that you can enjoy the produce it provides. There is no better food available than home-grown heirloom produce.
